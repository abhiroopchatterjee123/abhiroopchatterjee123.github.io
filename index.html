About Me

I am Abhiroop Chatterjee, and I am currently pursuing my Ph.D. at Jadavpur University. I consider myself extremely fortunate to work under the guidance of Prof. Susmita Ghosh, whose expertise and mentorship have been foundational to my academic journey and Prof. Ashish Ghosh from Indian Statistical Institute Calcutta (currently the Director of IIIT Bhubaneswar), whose insights continue to enrich my deeper understanding about the field. I have also had the privilege of working closely with Dr. Lance Fiondella (University of Massachusetts, Dartmouth, USA) and Dr. Emmett Ientilucci (Rochester Institute of Technology, New York, USA), whose varied perspectives have added valuable dimensions to my interdisciplinary research. Together, we form a vibrant and collaborative team focused on pushing the boundaries of deep learning and computer vision. My research operates at the confluence of self-supervised representation learning, transferable neural architectures, and uncertainty-aware deep inference, targeting high-stakes domains such as biomedical imaging and geospatial intelligence. Specifically, I am invested in architecting semantically aligned, robust, and generalizable deep models. I am particularly interested in designing scalable frameworks that integrate label-efficient active sampling, generative modeling of latent priors, and adversarial robustness mechanisms to address the inherent data heterogeneity and distributional ambiguities prevalent in real-world vision tasks. My long-term goal is to build intelligent, interpretable, and reliable deep learning models that can make a meaningful impact in healthcare and remote sensing applications. Welcome to my personal webpage, where I share my publications and ongoing projects.
