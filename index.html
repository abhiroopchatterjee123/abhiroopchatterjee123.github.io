About Me

I am Abhiroop Chatterjee, currently pursuing my Ph.D. at Jadavpur University. I consider myself extremely fortunate to work under the guidance of Prof. Susmita Ghosh, whose expertise and mentorship have been foundational to my academic journey. I am also grateful to collaborate closely with Prof. Ashish Ghosh from ISI Calcutta (currently the Director of IIIT Bhubaneswar), whose insights continue to enrich our research group. Together, we form a vibrant and interdisciplinary team focused on pushing the boundaries of deep learning and computer vision. My research operates at the confluence of self-supervised representation learning, transferable neural architectures, and uncertainty-aware deep inference, targeting high-stakes domains such as biomedical imaging and geospatial intelligence. Specifically, I am invested in architecting semantically-aligned, robust, and generalizable deep models. I am particularly interested in designing scalable frameworks that integrate label-efficient active sampling, generative modeling of latent priors, and adversarial robustness mechanisms to address the inherent data heterogeneity and distributional ambiguities prevalent in real-world vision tasks. My long-term goal is to build intelligent, interpretable, and reliable deep learning models that can make a meaningful impact in healthcare and remote sensing applications.
